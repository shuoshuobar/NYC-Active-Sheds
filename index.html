City Council Cracks Down on Sidewalk Sheds

NYC passes tougher limits to reduce sidewalk shed presence and improve streetscape

May 4, 2025

The number of active sidewalk sheds in New York City has dropped to 8,477 this week, after peaking at about one hundred fifty thousand last year. The decline follows the City Council’s recent push to address what many see as a permanent blight on city streets.

In late March, the Council passed new rules to limit how long sidewalk sheds can stay up. The standard shed permit is now cut from one year to three months, except in cases involving building demolition, alterations or new construction.

“These are all really important design changes for the city of New York that will mean that we will meet our safety needs while also looking like every other city that does not have a skyline filled with sheds and scaffolding,” the legislation’ leading sponsor, Manhattan Councilman Keith Powers, said at a press conference before the vote.

Methodology

This analysis is based on publicly available data from the NYC Department of Buildings sidewalk shed permit database, accessed on April 30, 2025. We examined 8,537 active sidewalk shed permits across all five boroughs, analyzing their installation dates, locations, and permit renewal histories.

To identify the oldest sidewalk sheds, we calculated the duration between initial permit issuance and the current date. The analysis of permit renewals was conducted by examining the DOB's permit renewal database, which tracks each instance of permit extension. Borough-level analysis was performed using geocoding based on permit addresses.
